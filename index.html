Furu Wei

@ Microsoft Reserach | Google Scholar | @ Linkedin
Dr. Furu Wei is a Senior Research Manager in Natural Language Computing group at Microsoft Research Asia, Beijing, China. Before joining MSRA-NLC in Nov. 2010, he has been a staff researcher at IBM Research - China (IBM CRL) since Jul. 2009. Furu received his Ph.D in Jun. 2009 from Department of Computer Science of Wuhan University. Before that, he received a B.Sc degree from the same department in 2004. Furu's research interests include natural language processing, information retrieval and machine learning. He is now working on many NLP problems, models, algorithms and innovations. Particularly,
  • Text Summarization and Natural Language Generation
  • Machine Reading (Comprehension)
  • Conversation Computing: research & development of key technologies for AI chatbots & agents. Rich experiences in building chatbot products (e.g. Tay) from scratch.
  • Sentiment Computing: Computational Study of Sentiments, Opinions, and Emotions (aka. Sentiment Analysis and Opinion Mining)
  • Open Domain Question Answering (Web/KB/Social QnA, Text QnA)
  • Deep Learning for NLP (e.g. Reading Comphrehension, Sentiment Analysis, Question Answering, Summarization, and Conversation)
My work on conversation/chatbot, question answering, sentiment analysis, machine reading comprehension and text summarization has been part of many Microsoft products and services, including AI Chatbots, Bing, Ads, Office, and the Microsoft Cognitive Service APIs.
Furu Wei was named to the first (2017) MIT Technology Review’s annual list of Innovators Under 35 China (MIT TR35 China) for significant contributions to natural language processing.
